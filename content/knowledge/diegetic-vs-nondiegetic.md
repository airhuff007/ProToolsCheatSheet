---
title: Diegetic vs. Non-Diegetic Sound
description: Understanding the two fundamental categories of sound in film and media.
---

## Definitions

### Diegetic Sound
Sound that exists **within the world of the story**. Characters on screen can hear it. It has a logical source within the scene.

**Examples:**
- Dialogue spoken by a character
- Footsteps, door slams, ambient room tone
- A radio playing music in the background of a scene
- A character's internal heartbeat (if treated as audible to them)

### Non-Diegetic Sound
Sound that exists **outside the story world**. Characters cannot hear it. It is added for the audience's benefit.

**Examples:**
- Film score / background music
- Voice-over narration not happening in real time
- Sound effects added for dramatic effect that have no on-screen source

## Why It Matters in Post Production

Understanding this distinction affects every creative and technical decision:

| Decision | Diegetic Consideration |
|----------|----------------------|
| Music editing | Is this score or source music? Different licensing/stem treatment. |
| Dialogue premix | Diegetic — must feel "in the room" |
| ADR | Must match the diegetic acoustics of the space |
| M&E (Music & Effects) mix | Non-diegetic music and diegetic effects must be on separate stems |

## The M&E Mix

For international delivery, studios require a **Music & Effects (M&E) mix** — a version of the mix with all dialogue removed, so foreign-language dialogue can be dubbed in. This requires every diegetic sound effect and non-diegetic music element to be trackable and separable.

## Ambiguous Cases

Some sounds blur the line:

- **Stingers** — a musical hit that coincides with an on-screen event (partially diegetic)
- **Mickeymousing** — music that directly mirrors on-screen action
- **Internal states** — hearing a character's thoughts or memories (often treated as non-diegetic even if represented as diegetic)

## Pro Tools Track Organization Tip

Label your session tracks clearly:
- `DX` — Dialogue (diegetic)
- `MX` — Music (usually non-diegetic score)
- `SFX` — Sound Effects (can be either)
- `BG` or `Amb` — Background/Ambience (diegetic)
- `Foley` — Diegetic performance-synced sound
