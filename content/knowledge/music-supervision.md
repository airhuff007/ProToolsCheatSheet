## What is Music Supervision?

Music supervision is the process of selecting, licensing, and placing pre-existing music in film, television, advertising, and other media. The music supervisor acts as the bridge between the creative team, the record labels/publishers, and the post production team.

## Key Responsibilities

- **Creative selection** — finding the right song for a scene based on mood, era, culture, and character
- **Licensing negotiation** — clearing sync and master rights from publishers and labels
- **Budget management** — balancing creative goals with licensing costs
- **Clearance research** — verifying copyright ownership and licensing availability
- **On-set/set supervision** — advising on playback, live performance scenes, or period-accurate music

## Types of Music in a Film/TV Project

| Type | Description | Licensing Required |
|------|-------------|-------------------|
| Score | Original music composed for the project | No (work for hire) |
| Source music | Pre-existing songs heard in the world of the story | Sync + Master license |
| Theme / Title | Opening/closing title music | May be original or licensed |
| Needle drop | A famous pre-existing song used at a specific moment | Sync + Master license |

## Licensing: Sync vs. Master

- **Sync license** — rights to synchronize the song to picture; granted by the **music publisher** (controls the composition/melody/lyrics).
- **Master license** — rights to use the specific **recording**; granted by the **record label** or artist who owns the master.

Both are required to use a song in your project. If you record your own version of a song, you only need a sync license.

## Music in Post Production

The music supervisor works closely with the sound team to ensure:
- Music is delivered at the correct sample rate and format
- Music stems are available for the M&E mix
- Temp music in the edit is replaced with cleared music before delivery
- Music is properly spotted in the **Music Spotting Notes** document

## Pro Tools Session Considerations

- Clearly label music tracks: `MX_Score_01`, `MX_Source_Diner`, etc.
- Keep score and source music on **separate tracks** — they go to different stems and have different licensing treatment.
- Timecode-lock music to picture from day one to avoid drift.
- Deliver music stems at the same spec as the full mix (48kHz/24-bit for broadcast/theatrical).
